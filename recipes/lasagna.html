Description

Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish known for its rich layers of flavors and textures. It consists of wide, flat pasta sheets stacked with alternating layers of a savory filling, which typically includes a hearty meat or vegetable sauce, creamy b√©chamel or ricotta cheese, and generous amounts of melted cheese, such as mozzarella and Parmesan. The sauce is often made with a slow-cooked blend of tomatoes, garlic, onions, and ground meat, such as beef or pork, seasoned with herbs like oregano and basil. Once assembled, the lasagna is baked until the top is golden brown and bubbling, creating a deliciously crispy crust while keeping the layers underneath tender and flavorful. This comforting and satisfying dish is a favorite in Italian cuisine and is enjoyed worldwide, often served as a main course for family gatherings and special occasions.

How to Make Lasagna

  1. Make the meat sauce
  2. Cook the noodles
  3. Layer the lasagna according to the recipes instructions
  4. Cover with foil and bake
  5. Let the lasagna rest before serving

How to Layer Lasagna

  1. Meat Sauce
  2. Noodles
  3. Ricotta mixture
  4. Mozzarella slices
  5. Meat sauce
  6. Parmesan cheese
  7. Repeat the layers, then top with the remaining Parmesan
